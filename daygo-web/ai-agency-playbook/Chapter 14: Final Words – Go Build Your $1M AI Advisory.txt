The Power of Standardization

Think McDonald's. Think Starbucks. Think franchises.

They don't succeed because every employee is a genius. They succeed because every step is standardized.

Same burger, same latte, same experience - anywhere in the world.

That's the mindset you need for your AI advisory business. Every assessment. Every roadmap. Every call. Standardized. Repeatable. Scalable.

Templates: Your Shortcuts to Speed

Templates are your best friend.

What do you template? Everything:

- AI Growth & Profit Assessment: The questions you ask, the forms you use, the report you deliver.

- AI Roadmap: The structure, the phases, the formatting.

- ROI Calculator: Pre-built formulas where you just plug in numbers.

- Proposal Deck: A one-page pitch with Promise → Process → Proof.

- Advisory Call Agenda: So every client meeting runs the same way.

With templates, you stop burning time and start looking polished and professional.

Playbooks: The "How-To" Guides

Playbooks go deeper than templates. They document how you do things - step by step.

For example:

- How to run a discovery call (script, questions, flow)
- How to deliver an AI Growth & Profit Assessment (timeline, steps, deliverables)
- How to onboard a new retainer client (emails, kickoff call, setup)
- How to run a monthly advisory session (agenda, talking points, follow-up)

Playbooks let you train others. They let you delegate. They let you scale without losing quality.

SOPs: The Glue That Holds It Together

SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures) are your internal rulebook.

They cover the boring stuff that keeps your business running:

- How to send invoices
- How to schedule calls
- How to store client files
- How to follow up after a proposal

SOPs don't make you money directly - but they save you from chaos as you grow.

Case Study: The Advisor Who Scaled to $80K/Month

One of our top advisors went from $0 to $80K/month in under 18 months.

His secret? He templatized everything from Day 1.

Every client got the same assessment. Every roadmap followed the same format. Every call had the same agenda.

When he hired his first junior advisor, onboarding took two days - because everything was documented.

That's leverage. That's scale. That's how you build a real business.

Example: The AI Growth & Profit Assessment

Here's how you package it with leverage:

- Template: A pre-built form with discovery questions.

- Playbook: A guide that tells you exactly how to run the assessment call, when to ask which questions, and how to present the findings.

The point is consistency, not complexity.

Build what you'll actually use. Then refine over time.
